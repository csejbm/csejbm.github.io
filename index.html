About

I grew up in Southern California with a love of all the natural attractions my home has to offer. This pushed me to attend the University of California, San Diego to study Environmental Science. Instead I took a Cognitive Science elective my freshman year and discovered my fascination for the human condition.

I took a particular interest in the Distributed Cognition framework and the role that interactions with culture and technology play in shaping the mind and its processes. I believe that the processes and tools we engage with are critical parts of our ability to shape the world around us.

I am excited to live in a world that is becoming increasingly connected through digital systems. The integration of information technology into our every day lives brings with it new promieses and challenges. The way we design these technologies has important consequences; small differences in cognitive load can cause friction over time or result in serious and sometimes lifethreatening errors.

I believe that by taking a human-centered approach to the world's problems we can design systems that simplify our lives by understanding on our natural tendencies as people and using them to create tools that extend the realm of individual creativity.

Property Portfolio iOS

The Property Capsule portfolio app allows customers to access and present enterprise data about properties they manage and lease. Traditionally, leasing professionals work with a variety of document types that require a massive overhaul every time property data is updated. The portfolio app allows for automated updates of all marketing materials from a cloud server. Many of our users relied on binders full of paper documents for years, and were skeptical of switching to a digital solution, additionally, the app provides complex tools for users to search, create filtered collections, annotate architectural models, access private data, and more, so we had to make discoverability a key design priority. Beyond the design of the UI, white-glove onboarding and access to knowleadgable customer support were key parts of increasing user adoption.

Property Asset Manager

The Asset Manager product provides property leasing professionals as well as investors powerful insight into their own property data, exposing patterns that would have previously been hidden in different spreadsheets and accounting systems. The user can visualize their data from different perspectives and filter and sort their sales and rent data alongside property metadata to render maps, charts, site plan heat-maps, and tables that help analyze the performance of their real estate assets. My focus on this project was to create a powerful data analysis tool that did more than just act as a pretty display page, but displayed the right information at the right time in order to tell a story about the property and its performance. A key value point is the recommendations engine, which allows users to take action on under-performing assets by contacting potential tenants that have been determined to be a good fit by a proprietarty match making algorithm.

JLL Tourpoint

The JLL Tourpoint app allows users to present property site tours in an iOS optimized format. Site tours are ways that leasing professionals propose new sites to their tenants, and involve the aggregation of many data points in a visual format. Traditional site tours can take days for a marketing professional to produce and are frozen time, but Tourpoint site tours can easily be created by administrators online in a matter of minutes to be distributed for viewing on multiple devices. Tourpoint digital site tours are interactive, collaborative, and always up to date. While almost all users saw the value in digital property tours, many simply could not see themselves abandoning their traditional methods unless certain peculiar requirements were met. Although we initially though speed and interactivity would be enough, ability to for deep customization and offline support quickly became important needs to meet during initial user acceptance testing. We had to make sure we provided users with tools to customize that were scalable across organizations, and ways to distribute information to recipients in their preferred style.

Got the Feels

Got the feels was a student project to create a social oriented data visualization tool for exploring Anxiety disorder statistics across and the various risk factors at play. Users were able to see the number recorded cases of anxiety disorder diagnoses charted on a map of San Diego and compare that with heatmaps showing demographics information like age, income, and education. One of the questions we struggled with was users should do once they have spent some time with the data; given that it was a social project we wanted to connect users and allow them to form supportive chat groups. Concerns with privacy and providing a platform for misguided help without the supervision of professionals became challenges we faced during research, and in the end we decided it would be better if it helped drive people to other existing professional support groups instead.

Social Cognition in Cetaceans

Recorded and analyzed examples of communication and social cognition in a pod of dolphins. Dolphins are extremely socially advanced creatures, and have powerful forms of communication that demonstrate signs of language. This project is focused on sprectral analysis of vocalizations time-coded against social interactive behavior. The social behaviors of interest we scored into several categories and were recorded over ten-second intervals for each in a pod of 13 dolphins across 7 camera angles. Analysis was then done to map vocalizations to individual dolphins based on a frequency signatures. Research in still ongoing to map different types of vocalizations to various social behaviors observed.

Property Marketplace

The property marketplace is a space for Businesses and Tenant Reps to find available proeprty for rent or sale. Information regarding commerial properties is disjointed across the websites of individual owners and developers, if they even have websites. The marketplace using a combined approach of working directly with landlord accounting systems, as well as scraping and consolidating from a variety of third party databases, to provide the most up to date information about all commercial properties in one place. In order to minimize the users search time and provide the highest quality leads the tool uses intelligent matching to analyze their existing locations (or a competitor's) and score search results accordingly. The UX challenge for this project was to deliver a complex set of data filters in an extremely simple package. In the end we decided to base the initial search results off two user fields that we determined were the minimum needed to deliver value, and allow users to open up the algorithm and change the filter weights if they wanted to get more advanced.
