About

I was born and raised in Southern California with a love of all the natural attractions my home has to offer.

I attended the University of California, San Diego where I received a B.S. in Cognitive Science. The Cognitive Science program is an interdisciplinary field of study which allowed me to take courses in Psychology, Neuroscience, Computer Science, Linguistics, and Philosophy. While in school I took a keen interest in the Distributed Cognition framework and the role that interactions with language, technology, art, and society play in shaping the mind and its processes.

I believe that by approaching challenges from a user-centered perspective we can design solutions that extend the realm of human ability and give people more time to engage in the world around them.
